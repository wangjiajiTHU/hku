Bio

Jiaji Wang joined the Department of Civil Engineering at the University of Hong Kong as Assistant Professor in Jan 2023. He obtained his Ph.D. from the laboratory of Steel and Composite Steel-Concrete Structures at Tsinghua University and served as JSPS postdoctoral fellow at Kyoto Unviersity. Enthusiastic about applying high-fidelity computational methods in structural analysis and design, Jiajiâ€™s researches are mainly focused on three topics: (1) High fidelity constitutive models for reinforced concrete and composite structures and (2) Seismic performance and design recommendations of RC shear walls and composite shear walls under tension-bending-shear load, (3) Physics-informed machine learning for structural engineering.

  • Data-driven and physics-informed operator learning for solving partial differential equations
  • Structural health monitoring and structural control with smart material and deep learning
  • Computational mechanics infusing finite element method and deep learning

  • Novel constitutive models for high fidelity simulation of structures)
